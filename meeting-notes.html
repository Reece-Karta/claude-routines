Foothold America Meeting – Initial intro regarding US entity setup
30 Jun 2026, 4:55 PM AEST
Reece Munro (Karta Healthcare) Lori (Foothold America)

Summary

  • Foothold America is a US-based firm helping foreign companies set up US entities; strong client base in the UK, Australia, and New Zealand. Lori is the primary contact, based between London and Oxford.
  • Karta's two largest US competitors are domestic, and several US distributors have already declined to engage over international payment/paperwork concerns — establishing a US entity aims to remove that friction.
  • Ultralife (battery manufacturer, 10-year partner) has a facility south of Houston, Texas with roughly a third of its space vacant — a potential warehouse/shipping location.
  • The new entity will be a subsidiary of Karta Healthcare Australia; Reece intends to relocate to the US on an L1A visa, with entity setup a prerequisite for that application.
  • Recommended structure: incorporate in Delaware, foreign-qualify to trade in Texas (Nexus requirement due to stock held there); Texas fees are higher but operationally suitable.
  • Foothold America's stage-one entity setup is $2,500 USD, covering state/federal (EIN) registration, governance documents, registered agent setup, and foreign qualification filing; timeline 2–3 weeks.
  • An ongoing entity management service was recommended to handle compliance, given US penalties for missed filings can run $10,000–$25,000 (vs. ~£100 in the UK).
  • Stage two (employment/PEO service for payroll, benefits, and HR compliance) will be needed once Karta starts hiring in the US; Foothold America is also a healthcare insurance broker.
  • Banking plan: start with a digital bank (Revolut or Mercury) as a stopgap, then move to a bricks-and-mortar bank (e.g. Bank of America, Chase, Wells Fargo) once ~12 months of trading history exists.
  • Intercompany transfer pricing (AU parent to US subsidiary) is outside Foothold America's scope and will be handled by a referred US CPA/tax advisor.
